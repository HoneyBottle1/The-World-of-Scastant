### Studies Peaks
The Studies Peaks used to be part of the larger chain of the Silent Peaks. But, because of the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Glifs Island/History#The Glifs Rip\|Glifs Rupture]], they were separated from the mainland, along with the rest of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Glifs Island/Glifs Island - Overview\|Glifs Island]]. Their modern name comes from the Homedonian archives, nestled along the northern face of the mountains.
These mountains are quite old, but are rightfully difficult to trek through, because of the sharp terrain. The top of the mountains are covered in snow. 
Average Height : ~ 4,700 m. 
Average Length : ~ 269 km
### Casbron Range
The Casbron Range is a southern mountain range, located in the south of the Weschamian Empire, near the coast of the Inner Ocean. It is broken up into a western side and an eastern side. These are older and greener mountains, with a lower general elevation. Shrubbery and trees can grow on the sides and on top of these mountains, which are also quite flatter due to their age. Paths wind to the top, and viewing platforms and observatories were built near the top ridges. 
Average Height : ~ 2,550 m
Average Length : ~ 403 km (West: 127 km | East: 245 km)
### Silent Peaks
The Silent Peaks are the larger part of the mountain chain that was broken up in the Glifs Rupture. They are therefore quite similar to the Studies Peak, with the stark difference that they are much taller and much wider. The rock is quite hard and steep, making it a difficult region to travel or mine through.
Average height : ~ 5,000 m
Average Length : ~ 286 km
### The Great Line
The Great Line is the longest contiguous mountain line in Scastant. It is also the highest mountain range on the continent. It is a wide range, allowing for multiple mountains to form next to each other, with deep valleys between them. They act as the main and most impenetrable defence in the East, blocking out assaults from the Brantimek Empire. It is the main theatre of most battles in the War in the East, and is home to many Azraine fortresses and battlements, small and large alike. Most of the mountains are covered in snow, and they are so tall they sometimes touch the clouds.
Average Height: ~ 9,350 m
Average Length: ~ 664 km
### Dragonling's Nest
Dragonling's Nest is a mysterious and unknown place. It is a mountain in the south of the Great Line, significantly taller than any other. It is a very steep mountain, that easily breaks through any cloud. On clear days, some can see what looks like structures on its peak. Legend says it is home to an ancient and primordial dragon, sleeping, waiting for his time to return to the lands. But nobody has ever managed to climb the mountain, and these are probably just stories. 
Average Height : ~ 10,100 m
### Sandrial Cliffs
The Sandrial Cliffs are large sandstone cliffs to the south of the world, within the Sultanate of Monaance. They suddenly rise up from the endless dunes of sand, tall in the sky. Many small paths wind through the treacherous terrain, falling and rising. On the western side, the cliffs reach the edge of the land and fall into the sea, slowly eroding the terrain with time. A village made up of archaeologists and miners was built along the walls deep within the ravines, in search of riches or knowledge. Rumours indeed talk of a hidden temple somewhere in the region, as old as the rocks themselves, but few are the ones who still seek it. The roots of the canyons are filled with copious amounts of valuable ores, sold in the whole world. Some people, in their search for adventure, climbed up the steep cliffside to reach the top, sometimes seemingly touching the clouds. From the reports of people who came back down alive, it is a vast and mostly flat land, with some vegetation, and active wildlife. 
Average Height: ~ 1,500 m
Average Length (mapped): ~ 291 km
### Rothebron Rise
In the north-eastern part of the world, the Rothebron Rise divides the continent between the dry region of the Valin plains and the marshes of Duskenvar. They are home to the longest rivers in the land, and also feed the many large lakes of Duskenvar. To the south, their height drops significantly, becoming barely taller than hills. 
Average Height: ~ 4,650 m (South: 1,100 m)
Average Length: ~ 417 km
