# Preamble

This document is hereby established through the joint deliberation of the seven members of the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Everseen Council/Government\|Inner Council]] of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Everseen Council/Everseen Council - Overview\|Everseen]], and ratified by ten of the twelve recognized Regions of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/General Overview\|Scastant]]. It sets forth the fundamental laws governing the use of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Science & Magic/Magic/Magic in Scastant\|Magic]], in the interest of preserving life, land, and balance throughout the world.
The articles herein define the rights, limits, and obligations attendant to the use of Magic. While the enforcement and action of these laws may vary according to regional authority, their legitimacy and supremacy shall be recognized across all lands of Scastant.
All persons dwelling within the bounds of Scastant are held to the knowledge and observance of these Laws. Any violation thereof shall be subject to judgment under the authority of a court of law.
This accord supersedes all prior regional or local statutes concerning Magic, and may, by due process, be amended or expanded as necessity demands.
# Article 1: On the Free Use of Magic

<u>Section 1:</u> Magic is an inherent aspect of all peoples of the mainland of Scastant. No individual, institution, or authority may forbid, suppress, or seize the natural use of Magic by another, save where such use violates the present Laws.
<u>Section 2:</u> No person shall, through threat, binding, coercion, or enforced submission, deny another the exercise of their Magic.
<u>Section 3:</u> The free use of Magic shall stand as a right, not a privilege, and shall not be abridged except in defense of life, land, and balance.
# Article 2: On the Prohibition of Temporal Manipulation

<u>Section 1:</u>  No Magic shall be used to alter, fracture, reverse, or otherwise alter the natural flow of Time.
<u>Section 2:</u> Any practice seeking to observe Time beyond present perception, or to draw knowledge from unrealized futures or erased pasts, is hereby forbidden
# Article 3: On the Integrity of the Earth

<u>Section 1:</u> Magic shall not be employed in such a manner as to permanently scar, hollow, poison, or exhaust the Earth beyond its capacity for natural recovery.
<u>Section 2:</u> The land, the waters, and all that grows from them are not to be treated as inexhaustible vessels of power.
<u>Section 3:</u> Any act of Magic whose effects endure beyond a generation shall be subject to scrutiny under this Law.
# Article 4: On the Binding of Magic to Objects

<u>Section 1:</u> Magic may be bound to vessels, tools, or crafted forms only for limited purpose and duration.
<u>Section 2:</u> No binding shall be made that acts independently of its creator's will or persists beyond their lifetime without express authorization.
<u>Section 3:</u> All such bindings must be declared, documented, and open to inspection by appointed authorities. 
# Article 5: On the Secrecy of Certain Arcane Knowledge

<u>Section 1:</u> Certain arcane truths, by their nature, imperil the world if widely known or carelessly pursued.
<u>Section 2:</u> The study, transcription, or dissemination of such knowledge may be lawfully restricted for the preservation of public safety.
<u>Section 3:</u> No restriction shall be imposed lightly, and all such determinations must be justified before the Inner Council.
# Article 6: On Magical Accountability

<u>Section 1:</u> Any harm caused through the use of Magic renders the caster accountable in a court of law, regardless of intent or ignorance.
<u>Section 2:</u> Those who wield Magic bear responsibility not only for their actions, but for the foreseeable consequences thereof.
<u>Section 3:</u> Restitution, correction, or punishment shall be determined according to the scale and permanence of the harm inflicted.
# Article 7: On the Limitation of Magical Armament

<u>Section 1:</u> No individual, faction, or nation shall amass stores of Magic solely for the purpose of warfare, conquest, or annihilation.
<u>Section 2:</u> The unchecked accumulation of magical weaponry shall be regarded as a threat to all peoples.
# Article 8: On the Autonomy of Non-Magical Peoples

<u>Section 1:</u> Peoples and cultures whose ways do not rely upon Magic shall not be compelled to adopt, depend upon, or submit to it.
<u>Section 2:</u> Their customs, governance, and means of survival are to be respected as equal to those shaped by Magic.
# Article 9: On the Study of Living Magic

<u>Section 1:</u> No experimentation shall be conducted upon living beings for the purpose of extracting, refining, or amplifying Magic.
<u>Section 2:</u> Life is not a vessel to be emptied, nor a resource to be consumed.
# Article 10: On Council Oversight and Obligation
<u>Section 1:</u> The Everseen Council, in all its forms, is bound by these Laws as are all other authorities.
<u>Section 2:</u> No decree, tradition, or necessity shall place the Council above the Law.
<u>Section 3:</u> To violate these Laws is to betray the trust placed in the Council by the peoples of Scastant.






Article 7.3 : Magical force may be prepared only in defense, deterrence, or the preservation of peace. 

Article 9.3 : All study of Magic must respect the integrity of the living form, whether of flesh, root, or spirit.
