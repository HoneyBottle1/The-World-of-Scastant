Ever since humans appeared on the Earth, evidence shows many languages have existed, especially during the early years of the world. However, not much is known of such languages, for few written records were made, and fewer still survived the ages. 
Furthermore, as population increased and international trade developped, the variety of languages and dialects thinned, to ultimately form a generalised common language, spoken throughout most of the continent. This language would be used to facilitate understanding between different cultures. Throughout the years, and especially during the rapid growth and industrialisation of the Everseen Reckoning era, this common language became the norm and quickly overtook local languages, which were no longer taught at schools, deemed too minor to be rellevant.
# Dialects
While most local languages are long gone and no longer used, some dialects still remain in specific parts of the world. These dialects are variations of both the common language and local languages, varying to different degrees from easily understandable, to utterly unintellegible.
