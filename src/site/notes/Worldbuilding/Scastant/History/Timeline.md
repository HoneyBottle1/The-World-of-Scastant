### Before Everseen Era:

- <u>??? BE:</u> Out of Nothingness is borne [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/General Overview#The Concepts\|Time]], and thus starts the history of the world. 
- <u>5874 BE:</u> Evolution brings [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Fauna & Flora/Fauna/The Thalenvael\|The Thalenvael]] to [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/General Overview#The Concepts\|Life]], and gives them sentience. They start spreading all over the world, which is still entirely covered in one entire ocean.
- <u>5702 BE:</u> The Thalenvael have created numerous settlements on the seabed. The ocean has evaporated enough for the peaks of the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Mountain Chains#The Great Line\|Great Line]] to start emerging from the water.
* <u>4224 BE:</u> Most of the eastern part of the continent is now exposed to the surface. The Thalenvael had already understood the impact of the evaporating ocean, and started to migrate to deeper areas. Only small settlements had to be left behind as ruins after water evaporated. 
* <u>3566 BE:</u> Part of the west lands are now emerged. These are mainly hills and mountain peaks. In the north, the peninsula which would later become the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Gredan Sylvarsen/Gredan Sylvarsen - Overview\|Gredan Sylvarsen]] is still a long island.
* <u>3498 BE:</u> [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Gredan Sylvarsen/History#The Awakening of Gaanlion\|Gaanlion]] awakens in the centre of the western island.
* <u>3498 - 3384 BP:</u> Gaanlion spends the next hundred years populating the island with more trees and imbues them with Life and sentience. 
* <u>3250 BP:</u> Evolution brings forth the first ever human beings on the newly emerged continent of Scastant. The last passage from the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Oceans, Seas, and bodies of water#Inner Ocean\|Inner Ocean]] to the greater western oceans is now sealed. 
- <u>3211 BE:</u> Most of the known world is now on the surface. The western island is now connected to the mainland through a small northern passage. Nomadic tribes settle along the Sylvarsen River, eventually forming the Gredan Sylvarsen. They find and worship Gaanlion as a deity.
- <u>2905 BE:</u> A large group of Thalenvael, made of multiple families, in their quest to seek deeper seabed, end up trapped at the bottom of the Inner Ocean.
- <u>2901 BE:</u> Far to the West, an exceptional hearing is held in the throne room of the original [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Homedons Northern Islands/History\|Homedons Islands]] concerning the overcrowding of the main island and its surroundings. No good solution is found.
* <u>2901 BE:</u> These Thalenvael trapped in the Inner Ocean learn to accept their impending doom, and create their last ever settlement, which grows to become the magnificent [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Inner Ocean/Environment/Cities & Towns/The Sunken City/Overview\|Sunken City]], the largest citadel that had ever been built by any Thalenvael since their awakening.
* <u>2640 BE:</u> The Homedonians face destruction because of the lack of resources and food for its whole population. The chieftains scramble for solutions.
* <u>2635 BE:</u> The Thalenvael trapped in the Inner Ocean finish construction on the Inner Ocean. 
* <u>2591 BE:</u> The Homedonians have to turn to their last resort, which is to leave in search of a new home. The plan is set to expand their already large fleet as much as possible in order to carry the most people away. The deadline is to leave in 25 years at most, after which, the population will have died in too high numbers. 
- <u>2568 BE:</u> Having scrapped as much materials as possible, from every tree, mines, and even buildings on the islands, most of the Homedonians leave on the generational ships for their last ditch effort in survival. They have prepared those ships to be able to attach at sea under specific conditions. Their greatest inventors have worked on a portable version of an earlier desalination device to make seawater drinkable. On each ship are multiple food beds for them to grow their own food (along with fishing). The ships are a marvel of engineering, and they set sail East, toward the sunset, on a fateful [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/General Overview#Seasons, Months and Calendars\|Late Dark Season]] day. 
- <u>2406 BE:</u> The Homedons vanguard anchors their ship for the first time in 162 years, after many generations. These few ships have landed on the island of Frostheim, one of the many Northern Islands of Scastant. 
- <u>2402 - 2383 BE:</u> The [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Homedons Northern Islands/Homedons Northern Islands - Overview\|Homedons Northern Islands]] are conquered and unified under the reign of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Stories/Characters/Vigrmóðr\|Vigrmóðr]], a Viking warlord, establishing the basis of their seafaring culture.
* <u>2387 BE:</u> Deep in the Inner Ocean, the trapped Thalenvael have mastered the tides and the powers of the Sea. With their newfound knowledge and power, they create a structure within their citadel to slow the evaporation of the oceans. 
- <u>1700 BE:</u> [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Valin Wastes/History#The Great Cataclysm\|The Great Cataclysm]], an unknown magical event, scorches the Northern Wastes and creates the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Valin Wastes/Valin Wastes - Overview\|Valin Wastes]]. Survivors migrated South and joined the settlements in the south, which would later become the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Tiondywarm Kingdom/Tiondywarm Kingdom - Overview\|Tiondywarm Kingdom]] and the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Weschamian Empire/Weschamian Empire - Overview\|Weschamian Empire]].
- <u>1324 BE:</u> Under a new ruler's reign, the Homedonians invade part of the mainland, to the north of the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Mountain Chains#Silent Peaks\|Silent Peaks]]. After threats of a global conflict with the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Monaance Sultanate/Monaance Sultanate - Overview\|Monaance Sultanate]] and the Weschamian Empire and negotiations, the Homedonians sign a treaty to stop their expanse further to the mainland. 
- <u>1320 BE:</u> [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Homedons Northern Islands/Environment/Cities & Towns/Virdenshølm/Overview\|Virdenshølm]] is built and becomes the Homedons capital.
- <u>1287 - 622 BP:</u> Construction begins near Virdenshølm of the great [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Homedons Northern Islands/Environment/Landmarks/The Oceanic Codex\|Oceanic Codex]], the largest and most imposing library in the whole continent, unmatched still to this day. It also doubles as a powerful lighthouse, built on the edge of the cliffs of the mainland of Scastant. It is the symbol of the power and wisdom of Homedons. 
- <u>153 BE:</u> A group of individuals made up of the wisest and most powerful Mages create the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Everseen Council/Everseen Council - Overview\|Everseen Council]], and start studying a way to control magic to stop the bloody conflicts of their time. They at first reside on the border of the Everseen Council and the Monaance Sultanate.
- <u>21 BE:</u> After a century of studying and inventing, the Everseen Mages finally create their first prototypes of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Everseen Council/History#The Creation of the Nexuses\|Nexuses]].
- <u>20 - 1 BE:</u> The mages of the Council sign treaties with the leaders of the Monaance Sultanate, the Weschamian Empire, and the Homedons Northern Islands to create their own region in the centre of their three nations. 
### Everseen Reckoning Era (ER)

- <u>0 ER:</u> The Everseen Council is established as the magical heart of Scastant. The mages create the Core Nexus, a massive reservoir of Magic, in their capital. 
- <u>7 - 357 ER:</u> Smaller [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Science & Magic/Magic/Astras & Nexuses\|Nexuses]] are built in each region, granting magic to everyone. During this same period, the [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Brantimek Empire/Brantimek Empire - Overview\|Brantimek Empire]] and [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Ashenval/Ashenval - Overview\|Ashenval]] refuse to be part of the Council's plan, either having other plans, or being too busy fighting each other to partake in diplomacy. 
- <u>241 ER:</u> A group of individuals from around the world form Wonderbit Co. and settle in the marshes and swamps of [[Worldbuilding/Scastant/Geography/Regions/Duskenvar/Duskenvar - Overview\|Duskenvar]]. As their enterprise grows, they start building towns that will then turn to cities.
- <u>251 ER - Present Days:</u> Astras, powerful stones through which magic can be channelled, start being manufactured, first by the newly-formed Wonderbit Corporation, then by more people across the world. As a way to limit the population's access to more powerful spells, the Everseen Council makes sure each Astra has a limit on how much power can be channelled through them. The most powerful of them are only allowed to be used by the Council and rich/powerful individuals.
- <u>288 - 435 ER:</u> The Brantimek Empire, thanks to spies in the west of Scastant, start manufacturing Forbidden Astras, which hold no limit on the magical powers being channelled through them. It however takes a very long time for them to develop these Astras, as they lack knowledge, funding and resources. In the end, they could only produce three of them before the Everseen Council launched an attack on the Brantimek Empire. The Council seized 2 of the Astras, the last one remaining with Brantimek's leader.
- <u>435 ER:</u> The Council finishes writing the first draft of the document that would impose limits and laws on the use of magic in the world, which would be signed by the majority of the time’s powers. This is the document that bans specific spells and qualifies them as Dark Magic. Before then, the laws in order regarding Magic were made by each specific region. 

- <u>467 ER:</u> One of the two Forbidden Astras is thrown out of the cart transporting them into the Inner Ocean, in the hopes of it never being found again. At some point, however, the sentient sea creatures find the Astra and expose it in their Museum of Surface Artifacts.

- <u>498 ER:</u> Explorers from the Tiondywarm Kingdom report sightings of a sunken city in the central lake, but their accounts are dismissed as folklore. 
* <u>522 ER:</u> The schematics, plans and methods regarding the manufacturing of Astras are leaked all over the world after a dispute between the leaders of the Wonderbit Co.v 
- <u>579 ER:</u> The sentient sea creatures suddenly disappear without leaving a trace. The mystery of their disappearance will remain unknown for centuries to come.

- <u>601 ER:</u> The Homedonians launch an invasion of Glifs Island but form an uneasy alliance with the islanders after realizing their lack of magic, and the potential of the lands.

- <u>824 ER:</u> The Kingdom of Monaance experiences a golden age, expanding its territory into the southern desertic regions.

- <u>999 ER:</u> The Brantimek Empire begins its conquest of the East. They will be qualified as "dark forces" as part of Everseen's propaganda. The Kingdom of Vangincouisa and Tam Azraine form an alliance to guard the Great Line.

- <u>1001 ER:</u> The rupture of Glifs Island. The Council organizes a plot to shatter the western mainland using forbidden magic, isolating the island. The Core Nexus's connection is severed, leading to the slow decline of magic on the newly-formed island.

- <u>1003 ER:</u> The population of Azraine is called to defend the Great Line. In this war effort, they build multiple fortresses along the mountain range, using most of the resources of their old homes.

- <u>1005 ER:</u> The Council closes its borders with the rest of the world in an isolationist policy.

- <u>1046 ER:</u> The defence of Vangincouisa falls after continuous and relentless attacks by the Brantimek's armies. The spiders scatter in the hopes of surviving. Some manage to escape through the Mountain Pass, while others are forced to hide in caves.

- <u>1050 ER:</u> The Khog Buldar Mountain Pass is closed to stop the Brantimek's advance in the region.

- <u>1177 ER:</u> The Azrainians are now unrecognizable from what they used to look like. 175 years of living in the underground darkness changed them, now looking like a Dwarf race.

- <u>1188 ER:</u> The Wonderbit Corporation, now having grown to a full-fledged company-driven country, foresees growing relationships between the Brantimek Empire and the Ashenval clan. They decide to build fortifications along the border with Ashenval, to prepare for an imminent attack.

- <u>1196 ER:</u> The Ashenval clan allies with the Brantimek Empire, strengthening the dark forces in the East. Their goal is to find a way to destroy the Core Nexus to remove the Council's magical control over the world. Most of the population of Duskenvar is called to defend the fortifications along the Ashenval border.
  
- <u>Present (1225 ER):</u> The Eastern forces are at their strongest. The defences of the Great Line are beginning to falter, and alliances across Scastant grow increasingly strained.
